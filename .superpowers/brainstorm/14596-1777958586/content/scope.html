A. Full OS Metaphor

Replace the site entirely. Boot animation, login screen, then a desktop with dock + draggable windows. Apps for About (Finder), Writing (Notes), Projects (Files), Terminal, Experience (Timeline), Contact (Mail), System Monitor. Spotlight (⌘K) search. Most ambitious, most distinctive.

Cost: SEO/deep links break (everything is one URL); mobile needs a separate fallback; biggest rewrite.

B. Desktop Landing → Real Routes

Best of both. Home page (/) shows an OS-style desktop with a dock and "app" icons that navigate to normal routes. /blogs, /projects, /cve-map stay full pages but inherit the dark/mono visual style. Optional terminal-as-an-app overlay anywhere.

Cost: URLs/SEO/deep links preserved; comments and existing routes survive; less novelty than A.

C. Aesthetic Only — Same Site, New Skin

Lowest risk. Keep all current pages, components, and routes. Restyle: dark navy palette, JetBrains Mono accents, terminal vibes, grid-paper background, orange/cyan accent, the "// label" pattern. Hero/sections look HEM-OS-y but it's still a normal scrolling site.

Cost: Smallest delta from where you are; loses the "wow this is a fake desktop" moment.

My recommendation: B. Hybrid — the desktop landing is the memorable thing, but you keep your blog URLs, comments, deep links, and the existing CVE games. It's the lowest-regret way to capture the HEM-OS feel without throwing away what you've already built.
